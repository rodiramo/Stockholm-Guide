Discover the Districts

Stockholm, built on 14 islands, is considered one of the most beautiful capitals in the world. From medieval buildings and lush green parks to modern skyscrapers and colorful 'funkis' buildings, learn more about the unique character of each district.

Brommaplan District

Bromma is located in the western part of Stockholm, which is part of the municipality of the same name.

Djurgärden District

A place appreciated by tourists, it houses several museums, a zoo, and the Gröna Lund amusement park.
The prince resides with his wife in an apartment in this district.

Gamla Stan

Gamla Stan is the Old Town of Stockholm in Sweden. The name literally means "The Old Town".

Södermalm

Forms the southern part of the city center. It is divided into two municipalities: Katarina-Sofia and Maria-Gamla Stan.
